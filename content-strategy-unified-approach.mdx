## Executive Summary

After analyzing the current customer and practitioner sections, I propose a unified information architecture that serves both audiences through a role-based navigation approach. This strategy addresses the core issue of audience overlap while maintaining content relevance for both groups.

## Current State Analysis

**Customer Section Focus:**
- Business outcomes and value propositions
- Delivery challenges and modernization obstacles
- Organizational fit and readiness assessment

**Practitioner Section Focus:**
- Individual practitioner fit and capabilities
- Working posture and decision-making approaches
- Technical and cultural expectations

**Key Overlap:**
Both sections address organizational transformation, capability development, and long-term sustainability. The distinction is in perspective (business vs. individual practitioner) rather than subject matter.

## Proposed Unified Information Architecture

### Primary Navigation Structure
```
/engage/
├── Organizations (formerly Customers)
├── Practitioners
└── Shared Principles
```

### Content Organization Framework
1. **Organizations** - Business-focused content addressing:
   - Value proposition and outcomes
   - Implementation approach and methodology
   - Fit assessment and readiness

2. **Practitioners** - Individual-focused content addressing:
   - Role expectations and capabilities
   - Working posture and decision frameworks
   - Career development and growth opportunities

3. **Shared Principles** - Common foundational content:
   - Core principles and values
   - Working methods and approaches
   - Case studies and outcomes

## URL Structure Proposal

- **Organizations Path:** `/engage/organizations/*`
  - `/engage/organizations/what-we-help-with`
  - `/engage/organizations/how-we-work`
  - `/engage/organizations/when-we-are-a-fit`

- **Practitioners Path:** `/engage/practitioners/*`
  - `/engage/practitioners/who-thrives-here`
  - `/engage/practitioners/how-we-think`
  - `/engage/practitioners/what-this-work-demands`

- **Shared Content:** `/engage/shared/*` or top-level access

## Content Migration Plan

### Phase 1: Infrastructure Setup
- Create new directory structure `/engage/`
- Establish URL redirects from old paths
- Implement role-based navigation

### Phase 2: Content Migration
- Move existing customer content to `/engage/organizations/`
- Move existing practitioner content to `/engage/practitioners/`
- Identify and relocate shared content to common areas

### Phase 3: Optimization & Enhancement
- Identify content gaps in unified approach
- Create cross-links between organization and practitioner content
- Develop transitional content for overlapping areas

## Navigation Structure Design

### Primary Navigation
```
Primary Nav: Approach | Engage | Cases | Playbook
Secondary Nav (under Engage): Organizations | Practitioners | Principles
```

### Contextual Navigation
Each section maintains its audience-specific navigation while providing clear pathways to related content in the other sections.
