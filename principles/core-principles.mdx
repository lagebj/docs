These principles define the stance. They are not decoration. They exist to keep the work from drifting into delivery theater, consultant dependency, or tool-led confusion.

<p className="gs-lead">
  The principle set is a practical operating boundary: keep ownership with the customer, modernize inside the real system, and choose moves teams can maintain under pressure.
</p>

## 1. Customer ownership always

The client team must own goals, backlog, design, operations, and system shape.

We can advise, pair, challenge, and help unblock. We do not become the hidden owner.

If ownership is absent, the engagement is weak from the start.

## 2. Advisory and enabling, not managerial

Influence should happen through reasoning, visible trade-offs, and real work.

The job is not to control the team. The job is to help the team become stronger and more capable in its own right.

## 3. Modernize where value already lives

Real systems already carry business logic, history, and operational meaning.

That means modernization should begin inside the current system, not in a parallel fantasy built somewhere else and migrated later.

## 4. Simplicity and reversibility first

Prefer moves that reduce complexity and lower the cost of being wrong.

This usually produces better speed over time than heavy solutions designed to look comprehensive.

## 5. Tools follow context

Tools are not transformation levers.

A tool is only useful if it fits the context, lowers friction, and has a clear future owner. A tool with no owner becomes debt.

## 6. Work in the open

Progress, blockers, design choices, and trade-offs should be visible.

This is how you resolve seams early and reduce false alignment.

## 7. Minimal footprint

Add only what improves the client’s ability to move and own the result.

The best move is often to reuse what already exists and remove what does not need to be there.

## 8. Exit is designed in

The outside unit is temporary. The client capability must remain.

If the work requires our continuous presence to function, the model has failed.
