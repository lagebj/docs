---
title: Anti-patterns
description: The recurring traps Grounded Systems is designed to reject.
---

These are the common ways modernization work becomes expensive, fragile, or dishonest.

## Feature factory drift

This happens when the outside team becomes valued mainly for shipping output.

### Signals

- the client stops participating deeply
- the outside team starts carrying delivery pressure alone
- progress depends on external people rather than internal growth

### Why it matters

The client may get motion, but not capability.

## Modernize elsewhere, migrate later

This happens when a new stack or platform is built beside the real system, with a migration promised for later.

### Signals

- heavy future-state diagrams
- weak connection to current operational reality
- handover burden growing quietly

### Why it matters

This often creates a second problem space instead of improving the first one.

## Premature tooling

This happens when tools are introduced before the real constraints are understood.

### Signals

- tool discussions arrive before ownership discussions
- the tool becomes the story
- local skills and existing platforms are ignored

### Why it matters

The organization inherits new cognitive load without real reduction in friction.

## No-exit consulting

This happens when the engagement has no credible taper path.

### Signals

- the client still waits for external decisions
- key routines pause when the external unit steps back
- “just a bit longer” becomes normal language

### Why it matters

Dependency becomes the product.

## Local success sold as systemic transformation

This happens when one team improves and leadership treats it as proof that the wider organization has changed.

### Signals

- success stories detached from actual replication
- no evidence of the conditions being reproduced elsewhere
- a local win turned into a broad narrative

### Why it matters

It creates false confidence and hides the real scale of the problem.

## Ritual imitation without ownership

This happens when teams copy visible practices but do not internalize the reasoning behind them.

### Signals

- ceremonies survive, but judgment does not
- language is copied, but trade-offs stay implicit
- teams revert under pressure

### Why it matters

The form survives, but the capability does not.
