---
title: FAQ
description: Short answers to common questions about the Grounded Systems model.
---

## Is this staff augmentation?

No.

The point is not to extend a client team with outside capacity. The point is to strengthen the client team’s own capability and reduce dependence over time.

## Is this a delivery model?

Not in the usual sense.

Delivery can be part of the work, but it is a means for transferring capability, not the main product.

## Do you prescribe tools or frameworks?

No.

Tools follow context. A tool is only useful if it fits the environment, reduces friction, and has a clear owner.

## Does this only apply to technology?

No.

Many of the hardest constraints are organizational. Workflows, decision structures, approvals, and ownership boundaries often matter as much as the technical system.

## How small is the embedded unit?

Usually small enough to stay coherent and senior enough to carry judgment well.

## When should this model not be used?

It should not be used when the client cannot own the result, when the real ask is external throughput, or when taper would be treated as failure.

## How do you measure success?

The strongest signals are client independence, clearer ownership, confidence in decision-making, and continued movement without outside prompting.

## What happens after exit?

The client team continues carrying the work.

If a new blocker appears later, a short return can make sense, but the goal is always to step back again.
