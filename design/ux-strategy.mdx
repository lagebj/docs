## Vision

Create a **user‑centric**, **scalable**, and **maintainable** documentation experience that helps both external customers and internal practitioners find information quickly, understand our value, and feel confident in using Grounded Systems.

## Guiding Principles

1. **Clarity First** – Content should be concise, scannable, and organized into logical hierarchies.
2. **Discoverability** – Robust search, clear navigation, and contextual links reduce friction.
3. **Consistency** – Uniform UI patterns, tone of voice, and visual language across all pages.
4. **Responsiveness** – Design adapts gracefully from mobile to wide‑screen desktops.
5. **Exit Strategy** – Provide clear pathways for users to transition from docs to product trials, support, or contact.

## User Journeys

### 1. Prospective Customer
- **Goal** – Understand the problem we solve and how to start a trial.
- **Path** – Landing page → Value proposition → Use‑case examples → Call‑to‑action → Sign‑up.
- **Key Touchpoints** – Hero section, case studies, FAQ, contact form.

### 2. New Practitioner (Internal)
- **Goal** – Onboard onto Grounded Systems processes.
- **Path** – Home → Practitioners intro → Playbook → Role‑specific guides → Checklists.
- **Key Touchpoints** – Playbook, checklists, decision‑patterns.

### 3. Existing Customer
- **Goal** – Find specific guidance or troubleshoot.
- **Path** – Search → Relevant article → Related resources → Support chat.
- **Key Touchpoints** – Search bar, breadcrumbs, related links.

## Information Architecture

- **Top‑Level Sections** (mirroring folder structure):
  - `customers/` – Audience‑focused marketing content.
  - `practitioners/` – Internal operational guidance.
  - `principles/` – Core philosophy and non‑negotiables.
  - `playbook/` – Field‑level processes and checklists.
  - `cases/` – Real‑world examples.
  - `reference/` – Glossary, FAQ, contribution guide, design system.

- **Navigation** – Persistent left sidebar on desktop, collapsible hamburger on mobile. Each top‑level item expands to its subsections.

## Interaction Design

- **Search** – Instant‑typeahead powered by Fuse.js, results grouped by section.
- **Feedback** – Inline “Was this helpful?” thumbs and a link to open a support ticket.\n- **Call‑to‑Action Buttons** – Primary (blue) for trial, secondary (slate) for docs download.
- **Responsive Tables** – Convert to scrollable cards on small screens.

## Visual Design (see Design System)

- Apply brand colors (`gs-brand-blue`, `gs-brand-slate`).
- Use the **Premium Callout** component for important notices.
- Maintain vertical rhythm with the `spacing-vertical` utility.

## Content Strategy

- **Tone** – Friendly, confident, and concise. Avoid jargon unless defined in the glossary.
- **Structure** – Start with a brief summary, then step‑by‑step guidance, ending with next steps.
- **Updates** – Follow the contribution workflow (`CONTRIBUTING.md`) to keep docs in sync with product releases.

## Metrics & Success Indicators

- **Search Success Rate** – % of searches leading to a page click.
- **Bounce Rate** – Low bounce on landing pages indicates relevance.
- **Conversion Rate** – Docs → trial sign‑ups.
- **User Satisfaction** – Feedback surveys after reading key articles.
