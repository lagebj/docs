## Brand Overview

Grounded Systems’ visual identity is built around a clear, trustworthy, and modern aesthetic that aligns with the **Mintlify** documentation platform. The brand serves two audiences:

1. **Customers** – external visitors who need to quickly understand our value proposition.
2. **Practitioners** – internal team members and partners who use the docs as a knowledge hub.

### Core Visual Elements

- **Primary Color** – `gs-brand-blue` (`#1e40af`). Used for headlines, call‑to‑action buttons, and key accents.
- **Secondary Slate** – `gs-brand-slate` (`#334155`). Applied to body copy, secondary UI elements, and background tones.
- **Border Muted** – `gs-border-muted` (`rgba(51, 65, 85, 0.2)`). Utilized for subtle dividers and input outlines.
- **Typography** – System UI font stack with a fallback to `Inter` for readability. Heading hierarchy follows **H1‑H5** with progressive scaling.
- **Logo** – The logo lives in `docs/images/logo-navbar.svg`; it should appear on the top navigation and the footer. For dark backgrounds, use the white version `logo-navbar-white.svg`.

### Usage Guidelines

- **Consistency** – All pages must use the brand colors from the Tailwind config (see `tailwind.config.js`).
- **Accessibility** – Ensure a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for text against its background.
- **Imagery** – Prefer simple line‑icons and monochrome illustrations that complement the color palette.

> **Note for developers** – The brand palette is defined in `tailwind.config.js` and can be referenced via Tailwind classes such as `text-gs-brand-blue`, `bg-gs-brand-slate`, and `border-gs-border-muted`.

## Brand Positioning

- **Purpose** – We provide authoritative, trustworthy, developer‑centric documentation that enables rapid onboarding and reduces friction.
- **Target Audiences** – Customers (prospects & existing), Practitioners (internal teams, partners), Contributors.
- **Value Proposition** – Clear, searchable, premium‑styled docs that turn technical detail into actionable knowledge, reinforcing confidence in Grounded Systems.

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Voice** – Confident, friendly, expert, pragmatic.
- **Tone** – Adjusts by context: instructional (clear & concise), persuasive (for marketing pages – enthusiastic), supportive (for support content – empathetic).
- **Guidelines** – Use active verbs, avoid jargon unless defined, keep sentences ≤ 20 words, favor "you" and "we".

## Messaging Pillars

- **Simplicity** – Information is presented in scannable chunks.
- **Credibility** – Evidence via data, case studies, references.
- **Premium Feel** – Use of callouts, premium badges, consistent visual treatment to signal high‑value content.

## Visual Guidelines Extensions

- **Iconography** – Simple line‑icons, stroke width 2 px, monochrome, use `text-gs-brand-slate` for fill.
- **Illustrations** – Minimalistic, monochrome or duotone with `gs-brand-blue` accent.
- **Spacing** – Use the `spacing-vertical` utility for vertical rhythm; maintain 1.5× line‑height for body copy.

## Copy Guidelines

- **Headings** – Title‑case, no period, limit to 6–8 words.
- **Paragraphs** – Begin with a benefit, follow with a step or detail, end with a CTA when appropriate.
- **Calls‑to‑Action** – Primary CTA uses `bg-gs-brand-blue` with white text; secondary uses `bg-gs-brand-slate` with blue text on hover.

## Brand Asset Usage

- **Logo** – Use the full‑color logo on white backgrounds; for dark backgrounds use the white version `logo-navbar-white.svg` (add future asset). Keep clear space at least `spacing-vertical` around the logo.
- **Colors** – Primary on interactive elements, secondary for background and secondary UI, muted borders for separators.
- **Components** – Apply `premium-callout` for high‑value sections; use the `Button` component styles defined in the design system.
