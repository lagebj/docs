# Paperclip Operating Doctrine Summary

This document provides an overview of the Paperclip Operating Doctrine, a comprehensive framework for conducting work that aligns with our core principles and promotes customer capability over dependency.

## Core Philosophy

Paperclip exists to help organizations make real systems easier to change, easier to own, and less dependent on outside help. We achieve this by working inside real environments, respecting existing value, and building customer capability.

## Foundational Principles

1. **Customer Ownership Always**: Customer teams retain control over goals, design, delivery, and operations
2. **Capability Over Dependency**: We prioritize moves that leave clients more able to carry work afterward
3. **Modernize In Place**: Legacy systems are value carriers, not waste to be discarded
4. **Simplicity and Reversibility First**: Prefer solutions that reduce complexity and can be easily undone
5. **Work in the Open**: Make reasoning visible and invite collaboration
6. **Exit Designed In**: Plan for tapered involvement and customer independence

## Operational Practices

Our day-to-day work follows practices that bring these principles to life:

- **Reading the Real System First**: Understanding actual conditions before proposing solutions
- **Following Structure Over Theater**: Addressing root causes rather than surface appearances
- **Solving for Durable Movement**: Creating lasting improvement rather than temporary fixes
- **Preferring Practical Truth**: Ensuring recommendations work under real constraints
- **Building Capability Continuously**: Increasing customer self-sufficiency in every interaction

## Decision-Making Frameworks

We use structured approaches for key decisions:

- **Technology Evaluation**: Choosing tools based on fit and sustainability
- **Engagement Scoping**: Defining projects based on mutual value and feasibility
- **Change Management**: Introducing improvements that respect existing structures
- **Risk Assessment**: Identifying and mitigating threats to customer outcomes
- **Solution Architecture**: Designing systems that increase rather than decrease capability

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

We actively guard against approaches that compromise our effectiveness:

- **Feature Factory Drift**: Measuring success by output volume rather than customer impact
- **Modernize Elsewhere, Migrate Later**: Building parallel systems instead of improving current ones
- **Premature Tooling**: Introducing tools before understanding context and ownership
- **No-Exit Consulting**: Creating dependencies rather than building independence
- **Ritual Imitation Without Ownership**: Copying practices without understanding their purpose
- **Local Success Sold as Systemic Change**: Mistaking limited improvements for broad transformation

## Context-Specific Guidelines

We adapt our approach to specific scenarios through guidelines for:

- **Client Engagement**: Conducting projects that build capability and respect ownership
- **Content Creation**: Producing materials that serve real needs in accessible ways
- **Strategy Development**: Creating initiatives aligned with customer contexts and capacities
- **Review Processes**: Providing feedback that improves outcomes and builds skills

## Implementation

This doctrine is not meant to be read once and forgotten. It should be:

- Consulted regularly during planning and decision-making
- Updated as we learn and evolve
- Referenced when onboarding new team members
- Used to resolve operational disagreements
- Challenged and refined through team discussions

By adhering to this doctrine, we ensure that our work consistently advances our mission of helping organizations strengthen their capability to manage and improve their own systems.
