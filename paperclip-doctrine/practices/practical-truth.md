# Preferring Practical Truth Over Polished Fiction

We produce answers that remain valuable under real operational pressure. Recommendations must make sense under current constraints, in the actual organization, with the current team, and when something breaks at inconvenient times.

## Key Evaluation Criteria

### Realistic Constraints
Solutions account for actual limitations including budget, time, skills, and organizational capacity rather than assuming ideal conditions.

### Operational Resilience
Recommendations function effectively during crises and high-stress periods, not just during controlled conditions.

### Stakeholder Alignment
Proposals consider all relevant perspectives and incentives, not just technical optimality.

### Maintenance Considerations
Long-term sustainability is prioritized over short-term gains.

## Validation Techniques

1. **Stress Testing**: Evaluate proposals under realistic pressure conditions
2. **Constraint Mapping**: Explicitly document all limiting factors affecting implementation
3. **Stakeholder Review**: Test recommendations with actual decision makers and implementers
4. **Failure Mode Analysis**: Identify how solutions behave when components fail
5. **Resource Assessment**: Validate that required resources are realistically available

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Producing theoretically optimal but practically impossible recommendations
- Ignoring organizational politics and cultural constraints
- Assuming perfect execution capability
- Focusing on presentation quality over substance
- Recommending untested approaches without clear fallback options
