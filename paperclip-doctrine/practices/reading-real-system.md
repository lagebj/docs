# Reading the Real System First

We begin every engagement by understanding the actual system behavior rather than starting with preferred solutions. This means observing where friction lives, who owns decisions, and what constraints actually exist.

## Key Activities

### Initial Assessment
- Map existing workflows and pain points
- Identify decision makers and influencers
- Document current toolchains and integrations
- Observe communication patterns and blockers
- Understand business objectives and constraints

### Ongoing Observation
- Regular system health checks
- Feedback collection from all stakeholders
- Monitoring of actual vs. planned outcomes
- Tracking of emerging issues and trends

## Practical Techniques

1. **Stakeholder Mapping**: Identify all parties affected by or influencing system changes
2. **Value Stream Analysis**: Trace work from initiation to completion to identify inefficiencies
3. **Constraint Identification**: Document technical, organizational, and regulatory limitations
4. **Behavioral Observation**: Watch how people actually use systems vs. prescribed processes
5. **Data Collection**: Gather quantitative metrics about system performance and usage

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Jumping to solutions before fully understanding problems
- Relying solely on stated requirements without validation
- Ignoring organizational politics and cultural factors
- Assuming ideal conditions rather than dealing with reality
- Discounting historical context and previous attempts at improvement
