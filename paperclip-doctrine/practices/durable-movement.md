# Solving for Durable Movement

We focus on changes that create lasting improvement rather than temporary fixes. Good moves improve the current system from within, teach something useful, make the next move easier, and increase confidence.

## Key Characteristics

### Internal Improvement
Changes happen within the existing system rather than requiring external replacements. We strengthen what exists rather than discarding it.

### Teaching Value
Every intervention should transfer knowledge that benefits the customer long-term, not just solve immediate problems.

### Enabling Future Progress
Good solutions remove barriers that would otherwise impede future improvements.

### Building Confidence
Successful interventions increase customer belief in their ability to drive change.

## Implementation Approaches

1. **Incremental Enhancements**: Make small changes that compound over time
2. **Skill Building**: Prioritize approaches that develop customer capabilities
3. **Process Integration**: Align improvements with existing workflows rather than disrupting them
4. **Risk Reduction**: Lower the cost of experimentation to encourage innovation
5. **Success Visualization**: Create clear evidence of improvement to maintain momentum

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Quick fixes that don't address underlying issues
- Over-engineering solutions beyond customer needs
- Creating dependencies on external expertise
- Focusing on impressive deliverables over meaningful impact
- Ignoring organizational capacity for change management
