# Following Structure Over Theater

We look for structural issues that cause recurring problems rather than focusing on surface-level activities. Real improvement comes from addressing underlying causes, not performing visible gestures.

## Key Indicators

### Structural Friction
- Recurring delays in similar processes
- Persistent miscommunication between teams
- Regular workarounds becoming institutionalized
- Bottlenecks that move rather than disappear
- Disconnected incentives causing coordination failures

### Hidden Ownership Issues
- Unclear decision authority
- Duplicate efforts across teams
- Blame cycles without resolution
- Unspoken agreements contradicting official policies
- Shadow processes developing to bypass obstacles

## Investigation Techniques

1. **Pattern Recognition**: Look for repeated failures or delays across different contexts
2. **Root Cause Analysis**: Use techniques like 5 Whys to trace problems to their origins
3. **Incentive Alignment Checks**: Examine whether reward structures support desired outcomes
4. **Communication Flow Mapping**: Trace how information actually moves through the organization
5. **Accountability Chain Tracing**: Identify who is responsible for what outcomes

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Addressing symptoms without examining root causes
- Focusing on effort visibility over outcome improvement
- Implementing popular methodologies without assessing fit
- Celebrating activity volume instead of value creation
- Ignoring political realities in favor of theoretical solutions
