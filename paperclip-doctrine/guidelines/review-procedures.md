# Review and Feedback Procedures

These procedures ensure consistent, constructive feedback on work products while maintaining our principles and standards.

## Review Principles

### Constructive Focus
- Feedback should improve outcomes rather than demonstrate knowledge
- Critique should be specific and actionable
- Positive examples should be highlighted alongside areas for improvement
- Reviewers should consider context and constraints

### Efficiency and Respect
- Reviews should be timely and focused
- Feedback should respect the effort invested in work
- Multiple rounds should converge rather than cycle indefinitely
- Review processes should not create bottlenecks

### Learning Orientation
- Reviews should transfer knowledge and build capability
- Feedback should explain reasoning behind suggestions
- Different perspectives should be welcomed and explored
- Mistakes should be treated as learning opportunities

## Review Process

### Preparation
- Reviewers should understand the context and objectives
- Authors should provide clear guidance on review focus areas
- Criteria should be established before review begins
- Timeframes should be agreed upon by all participants

### Execution
- Reviews should follow structured checklists or rubrics
- Feedback should distinguish between major and minor issues
- Questions should be asked to clarify rather than judge
- Alternative approaches should be explored collaboratively

### Follow-Up
- Authors should respond to feedback with specific actions
- Outcomes should be documented for future reference
- Lessons learned should be captured and shared
- Review processes should be evaluated for continuous improvement

## Quality Standards

### Alignment Check
- Does this work reflect our core principles?
- Is the approach consistent with our practices?
- Does it build capability rather than dependency?
- Is it designed for long-term success?

### Effectiveness Assessment
- Does this solve the intended problem?
- Is it appropriate for its audience and context?
- Can it be maintained and evolved by customers?
- Does it make future improvements easier?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Nitpicking details at the expense of bigger issues
- Providing feedback without sufficient context or understanding
- Focusing on personal preferences rather than objective criteria
- Creating endless review cycles without resolution
- Ignoring the learning and development aspects of review
