# Content Creation Standards

These standards ensure all content produced by Paperclip reflects our principles and serves our audiences effectively.

## Content Principles

### Audience Focus
- Write for real humans with actual jobs to do
- Address specific needs and questions rather than general topics
- Prioritize usefulness over completeness
- Consider accessibility and inclusivity in all materials

### Plain Language
- Use everyday words and short, direct sentences
- Explain technical concepts in accessible terms
- Avoid jargon, buzzwords, and inflated rhetoric
- Be specific rather than abstract

### Structural Clarity
- Begin with the most important information
- Use clear headings and logical organization
- Include specific examples and actionable guidance
- Connect content to real-world applications

## Writing Process

### Research and Planning
- Identify specific audience needs and questions
- Review existing content to avoid duplication
- Plan structure based on information priorities
- Define success criteria for each piece

### Drafting and Review
- Write multiple drafts focusing on different aspects
- Review for clarity, accuracy, and audience appropriateness
- Seek feedback from diverse perspectives
- Test content with representative users when possible

### Publication and Maintenance
- Publish content in appropriate formats and channels
- Monitor feedback and usage patterns
- Update content regularly to maintain relevance
- Remove outdated or unused materials promptly

## Quality Checks

- Does this content solve a real problem for its audience?
- Can someone act on this information immediately?
- Is the language clear to someone outside the topic area?
- Are examples concrete and relatable?
- Does this align with our principles of capability over dependency?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Producing content for content's sake
- Using technical language to sound impressive
- Creating overly broad or generic materials
- Focusing on search engine optimization over human needs
- Publishing without testing for comprehension and usefulness
