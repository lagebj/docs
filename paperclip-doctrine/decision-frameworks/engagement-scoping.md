# Engagement Scoping Framework

We define engagement boundaries based on customer needs, Paperclip capabilities, and success probability rather than revenue targets or availability constraints.

## Scoping Criteria

### Value Alignment


- Does this engagement advance customer capability meaningfully?
- Are customer objectives realistic and measurable?
- Do we have genuine expertise to contribute?

### Feasibility Assessment


- Can we achieve stated objectives with available resources?
- Are customer expectations aligned with likely outcomes?
- Do organizational dynamics support success?

### Strategic Fit


- Does this engagement align with our core principles?
- Will it contribute to our learning and growth?
- Does it reinforce or compromise our positioning?

### Resource Availability


- Do we have appropriate personnel for customer needs?
- Can we sustain engagement quality under constraints?
- Are competing priorities appropriately balanced?

## Scoping Process

1. **Initial Assessment**: Evaluate potential fit based on preliminary information
2. **Deep Dive Analysis**: Conduct detailed exploration of context and constraints
3. **Proposal Development**: Create scoped recommendations with realistic expectations
4. **Agreement Formation**: Establish mutual understanding of objectives and approaches
5. **Transition Planning**: Define handoff criteria from sales to delivery

## Decision Points

- Decline engagements that compromise our principles
- Structure engagements to maximize learning transfer
- Set realistic timelines and expectations
- Establish clear success metrics tied to customer capability
- Plan for gradual ownership transition throughout

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Overpromising to secure engagements
- Accepting all opportunities regardless of fit
- Ignoring organizational dynamics that impede success
- Focusing on revenue potential over strategic alignment
- Starting engagements without clear success criteria
