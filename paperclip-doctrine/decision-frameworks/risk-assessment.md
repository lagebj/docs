# Risk Assessment Framework

We identify, evaluate, and mitigate risks based on their impact to customer outcomes and our principles rather than generic probability assessments.

## Risk Categories

### Capability Risks
- Loss of knowledge when Paperclip personnel change
- Insufficient customer readiness for ownership transfer
- Dependencies on external specialists or vendors
- Skills gaps preventing independent operation

### Technical Risks
- System instability or performance degradation
- Security vulnerabilities or compliance gaps
- Integration failures with existing systems
- Technology obsolescence or vendor issues

### Organizational Risks
- Stakeholder resistance to proposed changes
- Misaligned expectations about outcomes or timelines
- Communication breakdowns affecting implementation
- Cultural conflicts with proposed approaches

### Financial Risks
- Budget overruns affecting customer commitment
- Unexpected costs creating project jeopardy
- ROI shortfalls undermining customer confidence
- Resource allocation conflicts with other priorities

## Assessment Process

1. **Risk Identification**: Brainstorm potential issues with stakeholders
2. **Impact Analysis**: Evaluate consequences for customer outcomes
3. **Probability Estimation**: Assess likelihood based on historical data
4. **Mitigation Planning**: Develop specific responses for high-priority risks
5. **Monitoring Setup**: Create tracking mechanisms for early warning signs

## Response Strategies

- **Avoid**: Eliminate risk by changing approach entirely
- **Accept**: Proceed with awareness and monitoring
- **Reduce**: Implement controls to lower probability or impact
- **Transfer**: Shift responsibility to better-equipped parties

## Decision Points

- Prioritize risks that threaten customer capability or independence
- Focus mitigation efforts on risks with high impact and reasonable probability
- Plan responses that increase rather than decrease customer self-sufficiency
- Communicate risks transparently with appropriate stakeholders
- Establish clear triggers for escalating concerns

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Treating all risks as equal regardless of impact
- Focusing on low-probability events while ignoring common issues
- Creating excessive bureaucracy around risk management
- Hiding concerns rather than addressing them proactively
- Shifting responsibility without ensuring capability transfer
