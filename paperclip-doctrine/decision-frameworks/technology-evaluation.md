# Technology Evaluation Framework

We evaluate technology choices based on their alignment with customer needs, operational constraints, and long-term sustainability rather than technical sophistication alone.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Fit with Context
- Does this technology match the customer's actual environment and constraints?
- Can it integrate with existing systems without excessive friction?
- Does it align with customer team skills and learning capacity?

### Ownership Clarity
- Who will own this technology after our engagement?
- Are clear operational responsibilities established?
- Does the customer have realistic plans for long-term maintenance?

### Risk Profile
- What are the failure modes and recovery paths?
- How does this technology affect system reliability?
- What happens when vendor support ends or changes direction?

### Cost Structure
- What are upfront, ongoing, and hidden costs?
- How does this compare to alternatives in total cost of ownership?
- Are costs aligned with expected benefits?

## Evaluation Process

1. **Context Mapping**: Document customer environment, constraints, and goals
2. **Option Generation**: Identify 2-4 viable alternatives including status quo
3. **Criteria Weighting**: Prioritize evaluation factors based on customer priorities
4. **Assessment Execution**: Score alternatives against weighted criteria
5. **Recommendation Validation**: Test recommendations against customer feedback

## Decision Points

- Choose technologies with clear ownership paths
- Prefer solutions with gradual adoption curves
- Select tools that reduce complexity rather than add it
- Favor approaches that increase customer capability
- Ensure any new technology has clear rollback options

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Technology-first recommendations without context analysis
- Choosing popular tools over appropriate ones
- Ignoring long-term operational implications
- Underestimating training and transition requirements
- Focusing on features rather than fit with actual needs
