# Work in the Open

Important reasoning should be visible near the work. We prefer visible trade-offs, clear ownership, and plain-language notes over hidden reasoning trapped in side channels.

## Key Implications

### Transparency Benefits
Open work builds trust, enables learning, and reduces coordination overhead. When reasoning is accessible, everyone makes better decisions.

### Document Culture
Documentation is not extra work but essential infrastructure for effective collaboration.

### Collective Intelligence
The best solutions emerge when diverse perspectives can contribute meaningfully.

## Practical Applications

1. **Decision Records**: Major architectural and strategic decisions are documented publicly with context and rationale
2. **Meeting Notes**: Key meeting outcomes are summarized and shared with relevant stakeholders
3. **Code Comments**: Implementation decisions are explained directly in code where appropriate
4. **Progress Tracking**: Work status is visible through shared dashboards and regular updates
5. **Feedback Loops**: Regular retrospectives and feedback collection mechanisms are institutionalized

## Communication Guidelines

- Explain the "why" behind decisions, not just the "what"
- Use plain language accessible to all stakeholders
- Make trade-offs explicit rather than implicit
- Link related documents and discussions
- Attribute decisions to responsible parties while encouraging collective ownership

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Hidden agendas or private decision-making processes
- "Magical" solutions without clear explanation
- Side-channel discussions that exclude relevant stakeholders
- Over-reliance on verbal communication without documentation
- Blaming individuals rather than examining systemic issues
