# Exit is Designed In

Paperclip engagements should taper over time. If continued success depends on permanent Paperclip presence, the model has drifted from its core purpose.

## Key Implications

### Sustainable Success
True success means customers can thrive independently after our engagement concludes.

### Gradual Transition
We actively plan for reduced involvement from the beginning of every engagement.

### Institutional Learning
Every engagement should contribute to customer self-sufficiency, regardless of duration.

## Practical Applications

1. **Engagement Planning**: Define success criteria that include customer independence
2. **Skill Development**: Prioritize customer learning over speed of delivery
3. **Documentation Standards**: Create materials customers can use long-term
4. **Transition Plans**: Develop explicit handoff strategies with milestones
5. **Alumni Networks**: Maintain connections for occasional check-ins without dependency

## Measuring Independence

Success indicators include:

- Reduced frequency of customer-initiated contact
- Increased customer team contributions to system evolution
- Successful handling of issues without Paperclip involvement
- Customer sharing their knowledge with others in their organization
- Smooth transitions when key Paperclip personnel rotate off projects

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Becoming indispensable rather than enabling independence
- Extending engagements unnecessarily for revenue reasons
- Creating systems that "require" specific Paperclip expertise to maintain
- Failing to document processes adequately for customer continuation
- Normalizing crisis-resolution patterns that prevent customer growth

## Continuous Improvement

Each engagement should inform refinements to our exit strategies, documenting lessons learned about successful independence-building techniques.
