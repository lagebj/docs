# Customer Ownership Always

In all our engagements, customer teams retain ownership of goals, backlog, design, delivery, operations, and system shape. Our role is to enhance their capability, not replace their agency.

## Key Implications

### Decision Making
Customers make decisions about priorities, trade-offs, and implementation approaches. We provide expertise, options, and recommendations, but the final call always rests with the customer team.

### Responsibility
While we bring expertise and effort, accountability for outcomes remains with the customer organization. We succeed together when they succeed independently.

### Knowledge Transfer
Every engagement should increase customer capability. We actively work to transfer skills, knowledge, and context continuously throughout our work together.

## Practical Applications

1. **Daily Standups**: Customers lead standup meetings and set agenda items
2. **Backlog Management**: Customers own the backlog, prioritize items, and define acceptance criteria
3. **Technical Decisions**: Customers have final say on technical implementation choices
4. **Team Structure**: Customer team members remain responsible for ongoing operations
5. **Documentation**: All documentation is written for and owned by the customer team

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Taking over customer responsibilities without explicit permission
- Making unilateral decisions about implementation details
- Creating undocumented "shadow" processes that bypass customer visibility
- Failing to explain reasoning behind recommendations
- Building dependency rather than capability
