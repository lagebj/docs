# Paperclip Operating Doctrine

This package contains the essential principles, practices, and guidelines that govern how we operate Paperclip internally. It serves as our operational constitution, defining not just what we do, but how we think about our work.

## Purpose

The Paperclip Operating Doctrine exists to:

1. Align team members on fundamental principles
2. Provide guidance for decision-making in ambiguous situations
3. Ensure consistency in our approach across different contexts
4. Preserve institutional knowledge about how we work
5. Enable new team members to quickly understand our operational culture

## Structure

The doctrine is organized into five main sections:

- **Principles**: Foundational beliefs that guide all our actions
- **Practices**: Concrete behaviors and methods we employ daily
- **Decision Frameworks**: Structured approaches to making complex choices
- **Anti-Patterns**: Common pitfalls we actively avoid
- **Guidelines**: Context-specific recommendations for various scenarios

Each section contains both overview documents and detailed specifications designed to be practically useful while remaining conceptually coherent.

## Usage

This doctrine is not meant to be read once and forgotten. It should be:

- Consulted regularly during planning and decision-making
- Updated as we learn and evolve
- Referenced when onboarding new team members
- Used as a reference point for resolving operational disagreements
- Challenged and refined through team discussions

Remember: The goal is not to constrain creativity but to focus it productively.
