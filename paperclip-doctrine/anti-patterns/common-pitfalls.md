# Common Anti-Patterns To Avoid

These patterns should be recognized and actively avoided in all Paperclip work.

## Feature Factory Drift

### Signs of Feature Factory Drift
- Focusing on shipping output rather than increasing customer capability
- Measuring success by volume of features delivered
- Prioritizing visible deliverables over lasting improvements
- Creating dependencies rather than reducing them

### Prevention of Feature Factory Drift
- Align all work with customer capability goals
- Measure success by customer independence and system health
- Focus on transferable skills and knowledge
- Design exit strategies into every engagement

## Modernize Elsewhere, Migrate Later

### Signs of Modernize Elsewhere Pattern
- Building parallel systems before improving existing ones
- Planning to replace rather than enhance current systems
- Creating second systems while neglecting current pain points
- Assuming migration will solve underlying architectural issues

### Prevention of Modernize Elsewhere Pattern
- Improve systems from within whenever possible
- Respect embedded value in existing implementations
- Address structural friction, not just surface symptoms
- Validate improvements in production contexts before expanding

## Premature Tooling

### Signs of Premature Tooling
- Recommending tools before understanding context
- Using tools as transformation levers rather than friction reducers
- Prioritizing technical solutions over organizational alignment
- Introducing complexity without clear ownership

### Prevention of Premature Tooling
- Understand context and ownership before suggesting tools
- Recommend tools only when they clearly reduce friction
- Ensure ownership is explicit for all tool-related decisions
- Validate that tools fit existing organizational structures

## No-Exit Consulting

### Signs of No-Exit Consulting
- Creating permanent dependency paths
- Becoming essential for ongoing operations
- Building systems that require outside specialists
- Neglecting knowledge transfer and capability development

### Prevention of No-Exit Consulting
- Plan for graceful handoff from the beginning
- Build in redundancy and clear documentation
- Train customer teams thoroughly
- Design systems for maintainability by generalists

## Ritual Imitation Without Ownership

### Signs of Ritual Imitation
- Copying practices without understanding reasoning
- Implementing ceremonies without addressing real problems
- Focusing on visible activities rather than meaningful outcomes
- Mistaking activity for progress

### Prevention of Ritual Imitation
- Understand the purpose behind practices before adoption
- Adapt approaches to real organizational needs
- Measure outcomes, not activities
- Continuously validate that rituals serve their intended purposes

## Local Success Sold As Systemic Change

### Signs of Local Success Misrepresentation
- Presenting single improvements as broad transformation
- Scaling local solutions without considering broader context
- Claiming organizational change from technical fixes
- Ignoring cross-cutting concerns and dependencies

### Prevention of Local Success Misrepresentation
- Acknowledge scope limitations honestly
- Consider broader system impacts
- Connect local improvements to larger objectives
- Validate that changes generalize beyond initial contexts
