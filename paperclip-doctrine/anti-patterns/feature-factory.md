# Feature Factory Drift

The tendency to measure success by volume of output rather than increase in customer capability. This leads to busy work that looks productive but adds little real value.

## Why It's Problematic

Feature factories prioritize shipping artifacts over solving real problems. They create the illusion of progress while accumulating technical debt and misaligned priorities.

## Signs of Feature Factory Drift

- Metrics focused on lines of code, tickets closed, or releases shipped
- Requirements accepted without questioning underlying assumptions
- Pressure to deliver "something" by arbitrary deadlines
- Customer feedback treated as obstacles rather than guidance
- Technical excellence sacrificed for faster delivery

## How to Avoid

- Measure success by customer outcomes, not output volume
- Question every requirement for its impact on customer capability
- Prioritize quality and sustainability over speed
- Engage customers in defining success criteria
- Regularly assess whether delivered features are actually used

## Recovery Strategies

- Pause feature development to assess current value
- Audit existing features for actual customer utility
- Redirect efforts toward capability-building activities
- Establish outcome-based success metrics
- Create feedback loops with end users of delivered features
